Threats: oil palm, mining & hydroelectric

Spatially explicit concession and infrastructure data covering the combined Sumatra/Borneo orangutan range area (plus a buffer, since pressure near a range boundary still matters). Oil palm and mining concessions are pulled directly from government registries via the Global Forest Watch Data API (Indonesia Ministry of Forestry / Ministry of Energy and Mineral Resources, among others — see each record's source); hydroelectric plant locations are from OpenStreetMap, supplemented with Wikidata to fill real coverage gaps (e.g. the Batang Toru/NSHE project — one of the most significant threats to Pongo tapanuliensis specifically, and OSM alone was missing key detail on it). This is government/community-sourced structured data, not a scraped/inferred claim, so it doesn't go through the human-review queue the Social tab uses — but it also isn't independently verified by this project; some permit-stage concessions cover very large administrative areas rather than an actual footprint. See scripts/fetch_threats.py.

All three layers are also on the Map tab — toggle "Oil palm concessions", "Mining concessions", or "Hydroelectric plants" in the layer control to see them spatially, with the same detail in each popup.
